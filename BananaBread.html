Banana Bread

I went to Boston to do my Masters over 25 years ago (gosh I am old!). One of my good friends, Whitney, used to make this incredible banana bread and bring it to class. She kindly shared the recipe with me.

I have to say that the recipe has never failed me. It has been a constant hit!

Ingredients

  • Butter - 1/2 cup or 1 stick of butter
  • Sugar - 1.5 cups
  • Eggs - 2
  • Buttermilk - 0.5 cup
  • Flour - 2 cups
  • Baking soda - 1.5 teaspoons
  • Baking powder - 1/4 teaspoons
  • Walnuts - chopped - 3/4 cup
  • Bananas - very ripe - 2 large or 3 medium bananas - mash them just before adding
  • Vanilla - 1 teaspoon

Instructions

  • Cream together the butter and sugar until light yellow and fluffy
  • Add eggs and buttermilk to the butter/sugar mixture
  • In a separate bowl, sift together the dry ingredients - flour, baking soda and baking powder
  • Add the sifted dry ingredients to the mixture. I do this in batches to allow the flour to mix in well, and to avoid getting flour all over me
  • Finally, add mashed bananas and vanilla
  • Add the prepared mixture to 2 loaf pans which have been greased with butter and floured
  • Bake at 180degrees for 1 hour. Bread is done when a toothpick comes out clean.
  • Move to cooling rack and allow the bread to cool completely before slicing. Enjoy!

Tips

  • Sometimes, when I am unable to find buttermilk, I have used slightly diluted unsweetened plain yoghurt, and the bread has tasted just as good!
  • It is really important when baking to use room temperature ingredients. I leave the butter, eggs and any other ingredients from the refridgerator outside to come to room temperature for at least 3-4 hours before baking.
  • This is a foolproof recipe. Honestly. Have made it so many times and it always comes out great.
