Sambar

Sambar is staple in every Tamil Brahmin household, where different variations are cooked every day to be eaten with hot rice and other side dishes. This is the version my mother taught me.

Ingredients

  • Half a small head of cauliflower, chopped into florets
  • Half a big carrot cut into half moons
  • 2 tomatoes cut into quarters
  • 1 potato peeled and cut into cubes (you can vary the vegetables but you need about 2 cups
  • Half a cup of cooked thovaram paruppu (cooked toor dal)
  • 1 lime size tamarind sokaed in 1/2 cup warm water for 15mins
  • 1.5 tablespoons sambar powder (i use homemade and will share recipe separately
  • Small piece of jaggery
  • Spices

For Tempering

  • 2 tsp oil
  • 1tsp mustard seeds
  • 2 pinches of fenugreek seeds
  • Pinch of perungayam or asafoetida

Instructions

  1. In a pressure cooker, combine thovaram paruppu, turmeric and add enough water to cover the paruppu. Close the cooker and cook until you hear one whistle
  2. In a big pot, add your tamarind solution, dilute it till pot is quarter full. Add a heaped teaspoon of salt and a pinch of tumeric. Let it boil.
  3. When it is boiling, add your vegetables and enough water to cover the vegetables and a bit of salt. Cook until vegetables are almost done
  4. Add the cooked thovaram paruppu and sambar powder at this point. Let it boil again
  5. Add jaggery, let it blend it. Test for seasoning and turn off the flame
  6. For tempering, heat the oil in a small pan. Add mustard seeds. Once it splutters, add rest of tempering ingredients, fry only for short while and add into sambar

Tips

  • Every family has a different way of cooking their sambar. You will find many recipes on the Internet. The key difference will be in the sambar powder used. The above method is by using a premade powder. There is another method with fresh ingredients. Recipe for another day.
  • You can any mix of vegetables for your sambar. Whatever vegetables you like, will work.
  • A pot of sambar made on a Sunday, can be saved/frozen and used for different meals including as side dish for dosai and idli.
